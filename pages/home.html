Research

I'm relatively new in the field of applied machine learning (ML) research, with an interest for computer vision, image processing, and generative AI.

Currently, I'm researching the application of diffusion models in clinical settings at the Xu Lab within the Computational Biology Department at Carnegie Mellon University, under the guidance of Dr. Xingjian Li.

My journey started as an ML researcher at Algoverse AI Research under the mentorship of Michael Lam and Kevin Zhu, graduate AI researchers from the University of California, Berkeley, where I had lead the authorship and a team of 4 other high school students to put our findings into an academic research paper.

Our paper was submitted and accepted for publication into the Workshop on Generative AI for Synthetic Medical Data (GAISynMeD) at the 17th biennial Asian Conference on Computer Vision (ACCV) in Hanoi, Vietnam. Please give it a read!

  • Medical Imaging Complexity and its Effects on GAN Performance
    William Cagas*, Chan Ko, Blake Hsiao, Shryuk Grandhi, Rishi Bhattacharya, Michael Lam†, Kevin Zhu†

    GAISynMeD @ ACCV, 2024 (Oral Presentation)

    We explore how increased complexity in medical imaging datasets leads to reduced GAN performance, and how this insight can guide efficient GAN training for medical image synthesis.
